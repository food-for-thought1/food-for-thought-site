Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) is a federal assistance program in the United States that provides low-income individuals and families with financial assistance to purchase food. It is the largest nutrition assistance program in the country, serving more than 40 million people each year.

Apply for SNAP

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (formerly known as “food stamps”) provides food assistance for low-income New Yorkers. SNAP helps families and individuals supplement the cost of their diet with nutritious foods.

NYC Food Pantries

Food pantries are organizations that provide food assistance to individuals and families who are experiencing food insecurity or struggling to make ends meet. Food pantries play an important role in addressing food insecurity and supporting the nutritional needs of individuals and families in their communities. They rely on the support of volunteers and donations to operate, and can be a valuable resource for those in need of food assistance.

NYC Community Fridges

Community fridges are open to anyone in need, with no questions asked about income, immigration status, or other personal information. They provide a valuable resource for people who are struggling to make ends meet, as well as a way for community members to come together and support one another. The NYC Community Fridge website provides a map and directory of community fridges in the city, as well as information on how to donate, volunteer, and support the movement.

Mutual Aid

Mutual aid initiatives for food insecurity can also be a valuable tool for addressing the root causes of food insecurity, such as poverty, inequality, and lack of access to resources. By building community solidarity and support, these initiatives can work to create more resilient and sustainable food systems, and can help to challenge the structural barriers that contribute to food insecurity.
